La Grave

The Scene

La Grave sits high in the Southern French Alps beneath the jagged face of La Meije, a mountain that dominates the skyline and sets the tone for everything that happens here. This isn’t a resort in the usual sense — it’s a wild, open mountainscape where the terrain has been left almost entirely untouched. The cable car lifts you straight into a world of rock, ice, and deep alpine silence, and from there the descent is yours to shape.

The Vertical Challenge

The mountain drops away for roughly 2,100 metres of vertical, running from glacier to valley in one long, continuous fall. There are no marked pistes, no grooming, and no comforting boundaries. Instead, the slopes are steep, varied, and unpredictable, with couloirs, gullies, powder fields, and exposed traverses that demand full attention. Every run is off‑piste, every line is chosen by the skier, and every descent feels like a small expedition in its own right.

A True Freeride Experience

La Grave has earned its reputation as one of the world’s great freeride destinations. Skiers come here for the challenge, the scale, and the sense of genuine mountain freedom — the kind you only get when the terrain is left in its natural state. It’s a place that rewards skill, confidence, and respect for the environment, offering a raw, unfiltered experience that stays with you long after you reach the bottom.
